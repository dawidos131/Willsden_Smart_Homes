Notes on compliance and larger works

I’m not registered with a Part P self-certification scheme, so I can’t self-certify notifiable domestic work. If a job is notifiable under Building Regulations (Part P) — typically new circuits, consumer unit replacements, or work in special locations like bathrooms — I can coordinate with a registered electrician so the correct compliance route and paperwork are handled properly.

If you’re unsure, send photos and details first and I’ll explain the options before any work begins.

Large projects like full rewires or major alterations are handled via a written quote, with a clear scope and schedule before work starts.

References: IET Part P FAQ, GOV.UK Approved Document P, Planning Portal Part P.

How we work

One point of contact, tidy work, and reliable cover when a job needs certification or when the diary is full.

1) Quick triage

Send your postcode, a short description, and 2–3 clear photos (plus any model numbers). I’ll confirm what’s likely involved and how long it usually takes.

2) Book the right person

Most small jobs are carried out by me (David). If your job needs a registered electrician for Part P compliance/certification, or if I’m fully booked, I can offer trusted local cover from my network — and you’ll know who is attending before anything is arranged.

3) Clear pricing

Small jobs follow the published call-out + hourly model. Larger multi-step projects are quoted in writing once scope is confirmed.

4) Compliance handled properly

If work is notifiable under Part P, the registered electrician handles the correct route and paperwork. If it’s a small non-notifiable job, work is checked and tested and I’ll explain what was done.

Data sharing (for job delivery)

If a trusted electrician is attending on our behalf or providing certification support, we may share your name, contact details, job address/postcode, and job notes with them for the purpose of delivering your request. See the privacy notice.
